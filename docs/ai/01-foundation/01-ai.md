# 1.1 What is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the field of computer science that focuses on building systems capable of performing tasks that normally require human intelligence.

These tasks include:

- Learning
- Reasoning
- Problem solving
- Understanding language
- Recognizing images
- Making decisions
- Planning

Unlike traditional software, AI systems are designed to adapt their behavior based on data rather than relying solely on manually written rules.

---

# 1.2 Why Do We Need AI?

Traditional software works well when every rule can be explicitly programmed.

Example:

```
if age >= 18:
    allow_vote()
else:
    reject()
```

However, many real-world problems cannot be solved using fixed rules.

For example:

- Recognizing a cat in an image
- Translating English to Vietnamese
- Detecting fraud
- Driving a car
- Writing software
- Answering questions

These problems involve uncertainty and enormous variability.

AI enables computers to learn patterns directly from data instead of requiring humans to define every rule.

# 1.4 Categories of AI

AI can be divided into several levels.

## Narrow AI (ANI)

Designed for a specific task.

Examples:

- Face recognition
- Chatbots
- Recommendation systems
- Spam detection

Almost every AI system today belongs to this category.

---

## Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)

An AI capable of performing any intellectual task that a human can perform.

Characteristics:

- Learn new tasks
- General reasoning
- Adapt to unfamiliar environments
- Transfer knowledge

AGI has not yet been achieved.

---

## Artificial Super Intelligence (ASI)

A hypothetical intelligence exceeding humans in every domain.

Currently, ASI remains a theoretical concept.

# 1.7 Common Misconceptions

## AI is just ChatGPT

False.

ChatGPT is one application built on top of Large Language Models.

AI is a much broader discipline.

---

## AI Thinks Like Humans

False.

AI does not possess consciousness, emotions, or true understanding.

It predicts outputs based on learned statistical patterns.

---

## AI Will Replace All Software Engineers

Unlikely.

AI automates repetitive tasks and increases productivity, but engineers remain responsible for system design, architecture, validation, security, and business decisions.

# Production Notes

As an AI Engineer, your job is **not** to build AI models from scratch.

Instead, you build systems around foundation models.

Typical responsibilities include:

- Prompt Engineering
- RAG
- Memory Systems
- Agent Architecture
- Tool Integration
- MCP
- Evaluation
- Deployment
- Monitoring

Model training is typically handled by organizations with access to massive datasets and compute infrastructure.

---

# Best Practices

- Understand AI before learning LLMs.
- Learn concepts before libraries.
- Focus on problem-solving rather than hype.
- Treat AI as a software engineering discipline.
